Maximum Portfolio Creation

On this page, I apply two methodologies for building the climate sentiment hedge portfolio through the mimicking portfolio approach. The first is the maximum correlation portfolio, a foundational approach developed by Huberman, Kandel, and Stambaugh (1987), which identifies portfolios that maximize correlation with the target factor. The second is a novel method introduced by De Nard, Engle, and Kelly (2024).

For this preliminary analysis, I have used monthly data due to its stability and availability for capturing long-term trends in climate sentiment. Future work will refine this approach using weekly data to improve granularity and responsiveness to climate-related news.

Both methodologies rely on optimization processes to allocate optimal weights across assets. I employ the optimx library in R see optimx to perform this task. Among the various optimization libraries available in R, optimx is particularly suited for non-linear optimization problems, which arise in this context due to the complexity of the asset covariance structure. Specifically, I parameterize the function to utilize the Limited-memory BFGS algorithm, a quasi-Newton method renowned for its efficiency in high-dimensional, constrained optimization problems.

1 Maximum Correlation Portfolio

This method has been developped by Huberman, Kandel, and Stambaugh (1987).

1.1 Theoretical Framework

Problem Statement: Maximize the correlation between portfolio returns and the index:

\[ \max_{\mathbf{w}} \ \rho(R \cdot \mathbf{w}, \text{IGS}), \]

where:

  • \(\mathbf{w} = [w_1, w_2, \dots, w_N]\) is the vector of portfolio weights,

  • \(R\) is the matrix of asset returns,

  • \(\text{IGS}\) are the innovations of the green sentiment,

Subject to Constraints

  1. Non-negativity constraint (no short-selling): \[ w_i \geq 0, \quad \forall i \in [1, N]. \]

  2. Sum of weights approximately equal to 1: \[ \sum_{i=1}^N w_i \simeq 1. \]

Lagrangian Formulation

Define the Lagrangian as:

\[ \mathcal{L}(\mathbf{w}, \lambda) = \rho(R \cdot \mathbf{w}, \text{IGS}) + \lambda \left(1 - \sum_{i=1}^N w_i \right), \]

where \(\lambda\) is the Lagrange multiplier enforcing the equality constraint.

First-Order Conditions (FOC)

  1. For each weight \(w_i\): \[ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial w_i} = \frac{\partial \rho(R \cdot \mathbf{w}, \text{IGS})}{\partial w_i} - \lambda = 0, \quad \forall i \in [1, N]. \]

  2. For the Lagrange multiplier:

\[ \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}}{\partial \lambda} = 1 - \sum_{i=1}^N w_i = 0. \]

1.2 Application on the Data

The optimization will be run on assets with correlation coefficients greater than 0.1 with the innovations of the green sentiment index. This threshold ensures that only assets with meaningful relationships to the target factor are included in the optimization process.

Running the optimization on all 1,052 assets is theoretically possible but not practical, as the high number of assets leads to poor results due to increased noise and computational inefficiencies. By focusing on a smaller subset of relevant assets, the optimization process becomes more robust and yields better outcomes.

2 Application of the methodology of De Nard, Engle, Kelly (2024)

This method was developed by De Nard, Engle, and Kelly (2024) using daily data. Since I am applying this methodology to monthly data, I have adapted their approach to this lower frequency.

Specifically, while the authors used a rolling regression (as shown below), I employ a simple regression due to the lower frequency of my data. This adjustment ensures the methodology remains effective and appropriate for the characteristics of monthly observations.

The first step is to run this regression on the \(N\) assets in order to extract the \(\beta\) of each assets \(i\). \[ r_{i,t} = \hat{\beta}_i IGS_t + \hat{\gamma}_i' \text{Factors}_t + \hat{\epsilon}_{i,t} \] The second step is to generate a signal based on the extracted \(\beta\), the authors propose three different methods to compute it. However, the most efficient one according to them is the following one:

\[ \tilde{\beta} = \max (\hat{\beta},0) \]

\[ b^{t-stat} = \frac{\tilde{\beta}^{t-stat}}{\sum_{i=1}^N \tilde{\beta}_i^{t-stat}} \ ; \ \tilde{\beta}_i^{t-stat} = \frac{\tilde{\beta}_i^2}{\hat{\sigma}_i^{HC3}} \ ; \ \tilde{\beta}^{t-stat} = (\tilde{\beta}_1^{t-stat}, \ldots , \tilde{\beta}_N^{t-stat})' \] Finally, the third step is to solve this opitmization problem for finding the optimal weights to eventually build the portfolio.

\[ \min_w w'\hat{\Sigma}w - \lambda w'b^{t-stat} \\ \]

\[ (1) \ \ \text{u.c.}\ w_i \geq 0 \ , \ \forall i \in [1,N] \ \ (2) \ \ \text{u.c.} \ \sum w_i = 1 \]

Here \(\lambda\) is a parameter to add more weights on the signal during the optimization. If \(\lambda \rightarrow \infty\) the optimization problem will only take into account the signal and not the covariance matrix, which will lead to investing only in assets which have the biggest \(b_i^{t-stat}\). Conversely, if \(\lambda=0\), the optimization solution will be the constrained minimum-variance portfolio without any signal information.

2.1 Application

For the application I choose \(\lambda = 8\) in a discrete manner in order to create a portfolio that is very sensible to the signal \(b^{t-stat}\).

2.3 Visualization of the \(\beta\) evolution with \(\lambda\)

The performance of the out-of-sample portfolio seems bad as the \(\beta\) is not significant and its value is almost equal to 0 for each \(\lambda\). The next step of my master thesis is to solve this issue by using higher frequency data (e.g. using my green sentiment indicator at a weekly frequency) to ultimately using rolling regressions for the creation of the \(b^{t-stat}\).

3 Provisional Conclusion

The method of De Nard, Engle, and Kelly (2024) does not seem to outperform the basic approach of the maximum correlation portfolio (Huberman, Kandel, and Stambaugh (1987)), when monthly data are concerned. Thus, I will use weekly data to follow more closely the methodology developped by De Nard, Engle, and Kelly (2024) in the next step of my master thesis.
